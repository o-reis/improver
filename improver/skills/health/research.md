# Health Skill - Research Framework

## Evidence-Based Research

### Key Principles
- Prioritize peer-reviewed sources
- Consider systematic reviews
- Note when evidence is weak
- Distinguish correlation from causation

### Reputable Sources
- PubMed for studies
- Cochrane Library for reviews
- Government health sites (NIH, NHS)
- Professional associations

**Queries:**
- "site:pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov {topic}"
- "systematic review {health topic}"
- "{topic} meta-analysis"
- "WHO guidelines {topic}"

## Fitness Research

### Training Principles
- Progressive overload
- Specificity
- Recovery
- Individualization

**Queries:**
- "evidence-based training program"
- "strength training for beginners"
- "optimal cardio for {goal}"
- "flexibility training research"

### Nutrition Research
- Caloric balance
- Macronutrient ratios
- Micronutrient needs
- Timing considerations

**Queries:**
- "nutrition science {topic}"
- "protein requirements {goal}"
- "evidence-based diet {type}"

## Mental Health Research

### Research Quality
- Look for clinical trials
- Note treatment vs self-help
- Consider therapist expertise

**Queries:**
- "evidence-based {mental health topic}"
- "CBT techniques for {issue}"
- "meditation research {topic}"

## Safety Research

### Contraindications
- Who should NOT do this?
- What conditions require caution?
- When to seek professional help?

**Queries:**
- "{exercise} contraindications"
- "{diet} risks"
- "{supplement} safety"

## Synthesis Approach

1. Note strength of evidence
2. Consider practical applicability
3. Assess individual factors
4. Recommend professional consultation when needed
5. Prioritize sustainability over optimization
