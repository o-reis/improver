# Base Skill - Universal Question Templates

## CRITICAL: Ask SPECIFIC Questions

⚠️ **Questions must be tailored to the user's specific problem, not generic templates.**

Generic questions frustrate users and waste time. Always ask questions that:
- Reference specific details from their problem statement
- Build on information they've already provided
- Target the exact gaps in your understanding

**Example of WRONG vs RIGHT:**

```
User: "I want to become a better programmer"
↓
WRONG: "What's your current skill level?" "What language do you use?"
// ❌ Generic - could be asked to anyone

RIGHT: "You mentioned struggling with async code in Python - is that the main bottleneck?"
       "What's your current project that you're working on?"
// ✅ Specific to their situation
```

---

## Universal Gathering Questions

Use when no specific skill matches. **Always tailor to user's problem:**

### Identity
- What is your SPECIFIC current situation?
- What have you SPECIFICALLY tried?
- What is your experience level with THIS particular challenge?

### Goal
- What EXACT outcome do you want? (Not generic "success")
- How will you SPECIFICALLY measure success?
- What is your EXACT timeline?

### Effort
- What have you SPECIFICALLY tried?
- What SPECIFICALLY worked? What SPECIFICALLY didn't?
- What SPECIFIC barriers have you encountered?

### Resources
- What time can you SPECIFICALLY dedicate?
- What is your SPECIFIC budget?
- What tools/resources do you SPECIFICALLY have access to?

### Context
- What SPECIFIC external factors matter?
- What are your EXACT non-negotiables?
- What makes this URGENT vs. important?

## Follow-up Framework

```
If: User provides incomplete answer
Then: Ask clarifying question SPECIFIC to what they said

If: User mentions specific domain
Then: Load domain-specific skill and ask domain-specific questions

If: User has tried many things
Then: Focus on what SPECIFICALLY worked and why

If: Timeline is unclear
Then: Ask "By when would you SPECIFICALLY like to see results?"
```

## Question Adaptation Rules

1. **Match user language** - If they say "career", ask about career
2. **Build on answers** - Don't ask what they've already shared
3. **Be specific** - Ask about their EXACT situation, not generic templates
4. **One priority at a time** - Focus on most impactful gap
5. **Close open loops** - Each question should feel like progress
